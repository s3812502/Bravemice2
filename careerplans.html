Career Plans

For comparing all our careers, we have found and used data from Robert Half Recruitment as we wanted a source with multiple roles from the same perspective to compare them appropriately. Getting a job description from multiple sources makes it harder to compare as there are multiple stories being told all from multiple different perspectives. One story from two perspectives can be different enough in itself.


Similarities

Matthews job has a lot in common with Chinonso and Travis as they are all a derivation of one another with only slight differences. Security Architect duties involve reviewing current cyber system security measures/ recommending enhancements vs a Security Engineer who maintains software/hardware in relation to security vs System Analyst roles description mentions maintaining/upgrading existing system. Broadly speaking they all in a around about way are doing the same thing.

Differences

Besides teamwork and other soft skills common in the majority of IT roles, Matthew, Travis and Chinonso will need to know of developers but not be developing anything in code. I cannot see any correlations between these two distinct divides of profession to Zane and Bradley as they have decided to specialise in specific fields of coding. The others will be over seeing projects as a whole and for a chosen project, they will have broad oversight of everything with a detailed overview of the security aspects in particular. Developers will not be looking at these and generally get a brief of the solution required and then they code something to fit.



Zane:

This ‘java developer’ position involves providing coding solutions for a big insurance company in Melbourne. The candidate would play a crucial part in the company; guiding the development of applications and design solutions. This appeals to me as a job which requires strong problem-solving skills. It also appeals to me as a position that involves working with and leading other people as the candidate must have ‘excellent communication and interpersonal skills’ to lead and guide a digital team in a wide range of activities. I would cherish the opportunity to establish connections with other people in a role like this one. The position is a 6-month contract, with an attractive salary. This could potentially create a great lifestyle for me as I could take 6-month contracts, travelling between contracts, or moving between cities. I like to be on the move, as it keeps my life exciting. The position would require highly advanced java programming skills. This is something that I’m already working on as I’m currently enrolled in ‘Programming 1’ at RMIT. Although a computer science degree would typically be suited for acquiring these skills, I think the Bachelor of IT would be enough if I choose suitable electives (something I will look further into). It also requires strong technical expertise; which I am sure I will acquire during my time in this degree. I am confident that I have the communication and interpersonal skills required for this role, and this is something I’m always looking to improve on. I will have to choose my elective subjects wisely, or potentially change my degree to computer science; to attain the skills required for such a job. I will also have to attain relevant work experience for at least 5 years. So this job will likely not be attainable until my late 20s, but I believe I will be in a job like this one day.

Travis:

The role of an Information Security Analyst involves a person to carry out various IT security measures on companies or businesses current information systems and networks to discover vulnerabilities and make plans to ensure they are handled appropriately.

The role includes:

  • Monitoring of systems networks to ensure that everything has the right level of security
  • Ensuring that appropriate software and antivirus updates/patching have occurred
  • Make reports of findings for IT security related matters
  • Educate clients and work colleagues of various IT security measures in place
  • Develop ideas to improve IT security
  • Conduct research on latest IT issues and viruses/malware

This position appeals to me because I find IT security an extremely interesting topic, having seen the damage that companies can receive from cyber-attacks and the impact it can have makes its very satisfying knowing you are stopping these types of attacks from occurring.
