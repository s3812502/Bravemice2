Skills Assessment — Matthew

Skills Required ( ranked most to least demand / significance ):

Soft Skills:

General:
  1. Critical thinking
  2. Open-mindedness
  3. Willingness to learn

Critical thinking will be highly required in this role as in this role I need to design secure networks and have to think of all avenues in which these systems could be compromised. This leads into the next skill of needing to have an open mind. Being naive in thinking it won’t happen or dismissing something on the likely hood it will happen can lead to possible security gaps in my designed system. New concepts, updates and or new technologies will always be appearing and implanting these will need to happen as soon as possible to fix or prevent future attacks.

Specialised:
  1. Detail-oriented
  2. Work well under pressure
  3. Oral communication skills

Delivering results in a timely manner would be critical to being successful in this career but these results must also be of quality. Presenting this information is often to non-technical parties and getting key points translated from technical jargon is a must.

Hard Skills:

General:
  1. Knowledge in architecture of computer operating systems
  2. High understanding of network Structure & Security
  3. University degree in IT

A brief overall view of all sides of IT provided by a generalised IT course. Advanced technical knowledge in key areas of networking/computer systems will be used to create advance secure systems.

Specialised:
  1. Master’s degree in network security
  2. Certifications like Certified Ethical Hacker

Specialising in security with the master’s degree will have prepared for the job role but also getting specialised industry certificates will narrow that knowledge down even further to vendor level
