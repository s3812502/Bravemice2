Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not?



Chinonso:

Having looked at the Burning Glass data my thoughts and opinions on a career within Cyber security still remain the same. Although the IT specific skills required in cyber security are not the most sought after with Microsoft windows skills being the only skill that was even ranked, I believe there is still a great demand for the job itself. Ranking at 13 with a total job posting of 2,914 it is clear that to land a position as a Cyber Security Analyst you have to be one of the best. The exclusivity of this career is what makes it so appealing to me, itll require the very best of me to be even considered. The higher wages of the career make up for the exclusivity of it with the medium wage being 100k according to seek job postings.


Matthew:

Looking at the burning glass data provides some insight however I will not be changing my choice of role.I have little to no interest in pursuing a full time role in programming. I will use some data however to learn the general skills in demand so I can do some professional development to enhance these areas.


Trevor:

Taking into the burning glass data and my choice to be an author or inventor its hard to use the data provided to make me want to change career direction within an IT sense as my aspirations are just not in IT. The data does show the industry could use more people with communication skills like mine however.


Travis:

Having read through the burning glass data, It has not changed my opinion on the role i hope to achieve it the future. What I have assertained through this data is the skills and experience required to perform the role and it has helped be define a clear path in order to achieve this.


Zane:

After viewing the burning glass data, I can see that a career as a java developer would be a safe one as there is a high demand for this skill. I am also still confident that I would be good at this position with the most important skills being problem solving, communication and creativity. I enjoy problem solving and although communication is not my greatest strength, I am always looking to improve in this area. So therefore my opinion about my ideal job has not changed, but I imagine it will probably evolve as I gain knowledge in a wide range of disciplines in my IT degree.


Bradley:

Considering the data from Burning Glass, it is clear that pursuing a career in Linux development is not amongst the most sought-after skills, however, there is still a clear demand for it. The listing for my ideal job specifies Java only, yet i think also learning JavaScript would broaden my skill set in web development toward more demanded skills by employers. Seeing SQL ranked at number 1 is encouraging knowing the ideal job i have chosen is one that involves skills that are in high demand, and motivates me toward focusing on learning or even specialising in SQL. Because SQL is in such high demand, and since cloud computing, which utilises SQL, is proving to be the direction that IT industries are heading towards, it stands to reason that an ideal job that involves both is a fairly safe bet and is not in need of changing any time soon.
